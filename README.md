## Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of **formal methods**, **cybersecurity**, **cyber-physical systems**, **spatial application** and **privacy**

I am particularly interested in:
- formal verification, model checking and logics
- attack graphs and attack-defense modeling
- Moving Target Defense (MTD)
- multi-agent reasoning and game-theoretic security
- resilient system design for adversarial environments
- cyber-physical systems (air, space, earth)
- privacy and become a numerical ghost

## Research Questions I Want to Explore

Some of the questions I am especially interested in include:

- How can realistic multi-step cyberattacks be captured in formally verifiable models?
- How can we assign realistic probabilities, timing, and costs to attacker actions in cyberattack models?
- How can proactive defenses such as Moving Target Defense be modeled, verified, and optimized?
- How can we reason about security strategies under many constraints?
- How can strategic and game-theoretic reasoning help defenders ?
- How can all these question or methods can be applied to something in the reality?

## Current Research Themes

My current work especially focuses on:
- probabilistic and timed logical formalisms for adversarial systems
- obstruction-oriented reasoning for attack and defense modeling
- formal reasoning about Moving Target Defense strategies
- verification of security properties in automotive cyber-physical systems
- bridging theoretical models with realistic system architectures and attack scenarios
